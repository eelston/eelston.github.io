In Spring 2023, I took 197DP, a course for hands-on experience which was described as follows:

  • Weekly hands-on electronics lab with term project. Weekly three-hour scheduled lab.
  • Additional two hours of studying/reading/prototyping expected outside of the scheduled lab time.
  • Electronic circuit schematic diagrams, circuit theory, LEDs, transistors, ICs (integrated circuits) and other electronic components.
  • Assembly language programming and microprocessor peripherals.
  • No prior experience with electronic circuits or computer programming required.


Through this course, I was introduced to assembly language and the KIM-1 clone, a board developed by Corsham Technologies replicating the MOS Technology 6502 single-board computer.
